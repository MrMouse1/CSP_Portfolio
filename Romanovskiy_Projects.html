    Project: AOI Circuit Design - Sentry Turret

    Course: DE (Digital Electronics)
    Duration: 3 weeks
    Objective: Create a circuit for a real-life application

    My team and I have explored different topics for this project until we stopped at the military protection of countries allocated near or within dangerous regions. Our design statement was to "create a defense system that can detect the difference between an enemy and anything else that may be moving." What stood out in this project, is what in comparison to other projects we have done before is step-by-step cooperation, where were not only the parts of the whole divided between team members but those segments follow each other one by one within the time limits.

    This project taught me not only to take care of my part of the job but also to pay attention to others; to make sure the team is working like clockwork.

    Project: PLD Design - 4 Bits Hexadecimal Counter

    Course: DE (Digital Electronics)
    Duration: 1 weeks
    Objective: Create a circuit for a real-life application

    After brainstorming for some time, I decided to make a translator from one numerical system to another. It was from binary system to hexadecimal system within one hexadecimal symbol (or sixteen bits). There was nothing new in creating this kind of circuit at the moment, so the most challenging part of this project was the troubleshooting due to a large number of segments. The project required calculations, documentation, and building a simulation, but transferring the simulation into an actual circuit, PLD, was the part that caused the most errors and difficulties.

    This project taught me to efficiently divide notable chunks of work and then manipulate it easily.
